1. Strategy Shift: Professional Execution

This strategy assumes the client is NOT a DIY marketer. Success for a premium experience business relies on high-quality storytelling, consistent engagement, and technical ad management. The client's role is Operator (delivering the experience); the Agency's role is Growth (filling the bookings).

Core Philosophy: "You drive the trucks; they drive the traffic."

2. Vancouver Marketing Agency Landscape

For an adventure tourism business, you need an agency that understands "stoke," seasonality, and the local outdoor culture. Avoid generic B2B agencies.

Top Tier: Full-Service Adventure Specialists

Best for: All-inclusive handling of brand, ads, and content.

  • Aventur Marketing: Vancouver-based, specializes strictly in tourism and outdoor recreation. Strong track record with tour operators.
  • EM Adventure Marketing: Deep focus on PR and influencer relations within the outdoor industry. Good for brand building.
  • Top Rope Media: Boutique agency with deep roots in climbing/skiing/outdoor culture. Authentic voice.
  • Cost Range: $4,000 - $8,000 / month retainer.

Mid Tier: Boutique Digital Agencies

Best for: Specific channel management (Ads + Social).

  • Major Tom (Small Biz arm): Larger agency with a polished approach to performance marketing.
  • Local Freelance Collectives: Teams of 2-3 freelancers (one copywriter, one ad buyer, one designer) working together.
  • Cost Range: $2,500 - $4,500 / month retainer.

Alternative: Senior Freelance Consultant

Best for: Budget-conscious specialized help.

  • One senior marketer handling strategy + contractors for execution.
  • Cost Range: $80 - $150 / hour or $1,500 - $3,000 / month retainer.

3. Agency Scope of Work

The agency is not just "posting on Instagram." They are the revenue engine.

Responsibility Agency Deliverables Client Responsibility
Social Media Content calendar, caption writing, reel editing, community management (replying to comments), scheduling. Capture raw iPhone video/photos during trips. Send "content dump" to agency weekly.
Paid Ads Setup & management of Google Ads (Search) and Meta Ads (IG/FB). Audience targeting, A/B testing, budget optimization. Approve ad spend budget. Provide high-res images for ads.
Influencers Identify targets, negotiate contracts, manage deliverables, track ROI. Host the influencers on the trip. Treat them like VIPs.
Email Marketing Design newsletters, set up automated booking flows (pre-trip/post-trip sequences). Provide customer lists (if any) and operational updates.
Reporting Monthly dashboard: CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost), ROAS (Return on Ad Spend), booking volume. Review reports and approve next month's focus.

4. Financial Breakdown

Budgeting for an agency is an investment, not an expense.

Monthly Retainer Estimates (Small Experience Business)

  • Management Fee: $3,000 - $5,000 / month
    • Includes: Social management (3-4 posts/week), Ad management, Monthly reporting, Email newsletter.
  • Ad Spend Budget: $1,500 - $3,000 / month
    • Paid directly to Google/Meta. This is the fuel for the engine. Start lower, scale as bookings come in.
  • Content Production: $500 - $1,500 / month (Variable)
    • Includes: Periodic professional shoots or paying for user-generated content (UGC).

One-Time Project Costs (Launch)

  • Brand Identity & Asset Kit: $3,000 - $5,000 (Logo, fonts, color palette, social templates).
  • Website Copy & SEO Setup: $2,000 - $4,000.
  • Launch Video Production: $3,000 - $6,000 (Professional promo video for website/ads).

Total Estimated Monthly Burn:

$4,500 - $8,000 (Agency Fee + Ad Spend)

5. Engagement Model: The 90-Day Roadmap

Do not sign a 12-month contract immediately. Start with a 3-month trial or "Launch Phase."

Phase 1: Pre-Launch (Months -2 to -1)

  • Focus: Brand Foundation & Teaser.
  • Agency Action:
    • Audit/Build Website SEO foundation.
    • Set up social handles and "Coming Soon" landing page.
    • Run small "Lead Gen" ads to build an email waiting list.
  • Cost: Project fees (~$5k) + Small retainer (~$2k).

Phase 2: Launch (Months 1-3)

  • Focus: Noise & Bookings.
  • Agency Action:
    • High-frequency posting (daily).
    • Influencer campaign execution (getting 5-10 influencers on trips).
    • Google Ads: aggressive bidding on "Vancouver adventure tours."
  • Cost: Full Retainer ($4k) + High Ad Spend ($3k).

Phase 3: Stabilization (Months 4+)

  • Focus: Efficiency & ROI.
  • Agency Action:
    • Optimize ads to lower CAC.
    • Reduce posting frequency to sustainable levels (3x/week).
    • Focus on email automation for reviews and referrals.
  • Cost: Full Retainer ($4k) + Maintenance Ad Spend ($1.5k).

6. DIY vs. Agency Comparison

Why spend the money?

Feature DIY (Client Handles It) Agency (Professional)
Time Cost 15-20 hours/week. Distracts from operations/safety. 1 hour/week (Strategy call).
Ad Performance "Boost Post" button (Inefficient, wastes money). Business Manager, Pixel tracking, Retargeting funnels (High ROI).
Content Quality Inconsistent phone photos, typos, missed posting days. Curated aesthetic, on-brand voice, consistent schedule.
Stress Level High. "I forgot to post today." Low. "I just need to run the tour."
Scalability Limited by owner's burnout. Unlimited. Agency scales spend as revenue grows.

7. Recommended Channel Strategy (Agency Executed)

1. Instagram & TikTok (The Showroom)

  • Agency Role: Edits raw footage into trending Reels. Engages with every comment. Hunts for potential customers via hashtags.
  • Client Role: "Documentarian." Film the mud, the laughs, the campfire. Upload to shared Google Drive.

2. Google Ads & SEO (The Cash Register)

  • Agency Role: Bids on high-intent keywords like "4x4 rentals Vancouver" or "unique camping BC." Manages Negative Keywords to save money.
  • Client Role: None. Just answer the phone/bookings.

3. Influencer Partnerships (The Social Proof)

  • Agency Role: Vets influencers (checks for fake followers). Negotiates deliverables (e.g., "1 Reel + 3 Stories"). Sends contracts.
  • Client Role: Host the experience. Ensure the influencer has a great time so their review is genuine.

8. Final Recommendation

Hire a "Boutique Adventure Specialist" Agency.

  • Why: You need someone who understands the product (overlanding is niche). A generic agency will waste money targeting "hotel tourists."
  • Budget Allocation: Set aside $20,000 for the first 3 months of marketing (Agency + Spend). This is startup capital, not operating expense.
  • Red Flags:
    • Agencies that promise specific booking numbers (impossible to guarantee).
    • Agencies that don't ask about your profit margins.
    • Agencies that want to own your ad account (YOU must own your data).
