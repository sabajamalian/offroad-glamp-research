2. Social Media Marketing Deep Dive

The strategy relies on "FOMO" (Fear Of Missing Out) and Visual Storytelling.

📸 Instagram (The Shop Window)

  • Content Strategy: High-quality aesthetic shots of the vehicles in epic BC locations. "Point of view" (POV) content from the driver's seat or tent window.
  • Reels vs. Feed:
    • Reels (Growth): 70% of effort. Trending audio + dramatic landscapes + quick cuts. Goal: Reach new eyes.
    • Feed (Gallery): 20% of effort. Carousel posts showing the "step-by-step" experience (The drive → The setup → The dinner → The view). Goal: Depth & Education.
    • Stories (Community): 10% of effort. Daily BTS, Q&A, reposting guest content. Goal: Trust & Booking.
  • Hashtags: Mix of broad (#ExploreBC #BeautifulBritishColumbia) and niche (#OverlandBC #RoofTopTentLiving #VancouverOffRoad).
  • Posting Schedule: 3-4 Reels/week, 2 Static posts/week, Daily Stories.

🎥 TikTok (The Viral Engine)

  • Content Types:
    • Raw & Unfiltered: "Things that went wrong" or "Reality of camping" (humanizes the brand).
    • Educational: "How to set up a roof top tent in 60 seconds."
    • ASMR: Sounds of rain on the tent, crackling fire, gravel crunching.
  • Growth Tactic: Hop on travel trends immediately. Use text overlays like "Did you know this spot is only 2 hours from Vancouver?" to hook locals.

▶️ YouTube (The Search Engine)

  • Strategy: "Hybrid Vlogs." Part cinematic travel film, part informational guide.
  • Content Ideas: "Complete Guide to Off-Roading near Squamish," "Toyota [Model] Overland Build Tour," "Camping in the Rain: Vancouver Edition."
  • SEO: Titles must answer specific questions users type into Google.

👥 Facebook & Google Business Profile

  • Facebook: Join groups like "Camping BC," "4WD ABC," and "Overland Canada." Share helpful advice, not just ads.
  • Google Business Profile (GBP): CRITICAL for Local SEO.
    • Post weekly updates (photos/offers).
    • Get 5-star reviews immediately. Reply to every single one.
    • Add "Products" with pricing for your tours directly on the profile.

3. SEO Strategy (Search Engine Optimization)

Focus on Local Intent and Niche Specificity.

Key Search Terms (Keywords)

  • Primary: "Off road tours Vancouver," "Car camping rental Vancouver," "Overland trips BC," "Guided 4x4 tours Vancouver."
  • Secondary: "Rooftop tent camping BC," "Adventure getaways from Vancouver," "Unique camping experiences BC."

Content Marketing Topics (Blog)

  1. "5 Best Off-Road Trails Near Vancouver for Beginners" (Capture top-of-funnel interest)
  2. "What to Pack for an Overland Trip in BC" (Helpful utility content)
  3. "Roof Top Tent vs. Ground Tent: Why We Choose RTTs" (Product education)
  4. "Hidden Gem Campsites in Squamish Valley" (Local authority)

Local SEO Checklist

  • Claim GBP and verify address.
  • Ensure NAP (Name, Address, Phone) consistency across all directories (TripAdvisor, Yelp, YellowPages).
  • Embed a Google Map of "Meeting Point" on the contact page.

4. Partnership & Referral Strategies

🤝 Tourism Boards

  • Destination Vancouver & Destination BC: Apply for membership. Goal: Get listed on their "Things to Do" pages.
  • Indigenous Tourism BC: Explore alignment and potential for cultural storytelling integration if appropriate.

🏨 Hotel/Hostel Partnerships

  • Target boutique hotels and high-end hostels (e.g., The Burrard, Samesun).
  • Offer: "Concierge Commission" (10-15% per booking) or exclusive guest discount codes.
  • Pitch: "We offer the 'Wild BC' experience your guests ask for, without you needing to manage gear."

🏢 Corporate & Gift Market

  • Corporate: Position as "Executive Team Building" or "Leadership Retreats." Focus on disconnection and resilience.
  • Gifts: Create beautiful digital/physical gift cards. "Give the gift of Adventure." Push heavily in Nov/Dec.

5. Paid Advertising (PPC)

Google Ads

  • Strategy: "Sniper" targeting. Only bid on high-intent keywords (e.g., "book off road tour"). Avoid broad terms like "camping" (too competitive/vague).
  • Budget: Start small ($500/mo).
  • CPC Target: $2.00 - $5.00.
  • Retargeting: Essential. Show ads to people who visited the "Booking" page but didn't pay.

Social Ads (Meta)

  • Creative: Video is non-negotiable. Use UGC-style clips (looks like a friend's story, not an ad).
  • Targeting:
    • Interests: North Face, Patagonia, REI, MEC, Toyota 4Runner, Overlanding.
    • Location: Vancouver + 50km radius (for locals) AND Seattle/Portland (for tourists).
    • Lookalikes: Create audiences similar to your Instagram engagers.

6. Marketing Budget Allocation

Phase 1: Launch (Months 1-6)

Goal: Awareness & Initial Social Proof.
Budget: ~15-20% of projected revenue (Aggressive).

  • Content Production (40%): Photography, videography, editing. You need assets to sell.
  • Social Ads (30%): Jumpstarting reach.
  • Influencer/Collabs (20%): Barter trips for posts. (Cost is mostly fuel/time).
  • Software/Tools (10%): Booking platform, email marketing tool, Canva.

Phase 2: Growth (Months 6-18)

Goal: Efficiency & Scale.
Budget: ~10-12% of revenue.

  • Paid Ads (50%): Scaling what works (Google & Social).
  • SEO/Content (25%): Blogging, link building.
  • Partnerships (15%): Commissions, printed materials for hotels.
  • Retention/Email (10%): Driving repeat bookings and referrals.

7. Recommended Marketing Path (The "Inara" Plan)

Top 3 Channels to Start

  1. Instagram (Organic + Reels): It's your portfolio. If it doesn't look cool here, it doesn't exist.
  2. Google Business Profile: The lowest hanging fruit for trust and local search.
  3. Micro-Influencer Seeding: Trade midweek trips for content. You need real people in your tents to prove it's safe and fun.

90-Day Launch Plan

Month 1: Foundation & Hype

  • [ ] Build "link in bio" with mailing list signup (Lead Magnet: "BC Off-Road Packing List").
  • [ ] Film 10 "Stock" Reels (scenery, gear, setup).
  • [ ] Claim & Optimize Google Business Profile.
  • [ ] Soft Launch: Invite friends/family for "test runs" in exchange for Google Reviews & Photos.

Month 2: The "Noise" Phase

  • [ ] Launch website.
  • [ ] Daily posting on Instagram/TikTok.
  • [ ] Host 3 local micro-influencers (5k-50k followers).
  • [ ] Turn on Google Ads (Low budget, high intent).

Month 3: Optimization & Partnerships

  • [ ] Analyze social metrics: What content got the most saves/shares? Make more of that.
  • [ ] Visit 5 local boutique hotels to drop off rack cards/pitch concierges.
  • [ ] Launch "Summer Booking" Early Bird email campaign.

Key Metrics to Track

  • CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost): Total Ad Spend / # of New Bookings.
  • Engagement Rate: (Likes + Comments + Shares) / Followers.
  • Website Conversion Rate: Bookings / Website Visitors.
  • Review Volume: Number of new 5-star reviews per month.
