Executive Summary

Business Concept

A guided off-road driving and overnight camping experience serving small groups (2–5 guests) in the Vancouver/Sea-to-Sky region. Using Toyota 4x4 vehicles equipped with rooftop tents, we provide 2-day/1-night adventures that combine hands-on off-road driving instruction with curated backcountry camping—all fully guided and all-inclusive (vehicle, gear, meals, instruction).

Market Opportunity

Vancouver's adventure tourism market generates $9.6B annually with 11.27M overnight visitors. BC's adventure tourism sector contributes $10.7B to the provincial economy. Despite this scale, no operator currently offers guided overnight off-road experiences near Vancouver—the market is split between day-only ATV tours and self-drive overland rentals. This business fills that gap.

Target Market

  • Primary: Couples and small friend groups (ages 25–50) seeking unique weekend adventures
  • Secondary: Corporate groups for team-building, gift purchasers, solo travelers joining shared trips
  • Geographic: Vancouver residents (60%), Pacific Northwest tourists—Seattle/Portland (25%), international visitors (15%)

Competitive Advantage

First-mover in guided overnight off-road + camping near Vancouver. We combine the convenience and expertise of guided tours with the immersion and adventure of multi-day overlanding, targeting customers who want authentic experiences without owning gear or planning logistics.

Financial Highlights

Startup Costs: $35,000–$50,000

  • Vehicle modifications & equipment: $20,000–$30,000

Break-Even: 25–30 trips (achievable in Months 3–6 with effective marketing)

Path to Profitability: Launch Month 1, break even Month 4–6, achieve 60–80 trips/year by end of Year 1, scale to 120+ trips/year in Year 2 with potential second vehicle addition.

1. Company Overview

Business Description

We operate guided off-road driving and camping experiences in the backcountry surrounding Vancouver, British Columbia. Guests join small-group trips (maximum 5 people) where they learn basic off-road driving skills, navigate scenic trails under expert guidance, and camp overnight in rooftop tents at curated wilderness locations. We handle all logistics: vehicle, gear, food, campsite selection, safety protocols, and instruction.

Mission

To make authentic backcountry adventure accessible to everyone—combining the thrill of off-road driving with the wonder of wilderness camping, without requiring gear ownership, technical expertise, or complex planning.

Vision

To become the premier guided off-road experience provider in British Columbia, known for small-group intimacy, safety excellence, and unforgettable backcountry storytelling.

Core Values

  • Safety First: Every decision prioritizes guest and environmental safety
  • Leave No Trace: Responsible wilderness stewardship in all operations
  • Skill Building: Guests leave more confident and capable than they arrived
  • Authentic Experiences: Real trails, real camping, real connection to nature
  • Inclusivity: Welcoming beginners and experienced adventurers alike

Legal Structure Considerations

Recommended: Incorporate as a BC Corporation or register as a sole proprietorship initially.

Key Considerations:

  • Liability protection: Incorporation shields personal assets from business liabilities (critical for adventure tourism)
  • Insurance requirements: Commercial general liability insurance (minimum $2M) and commercial vehicle insurance required regardless of structure
  • Tax implications: Consult accountant on GST/PST collection and remittance, business expense deductions
  • Business licenses: Require Vancouver business license if operating from Vancouver address; check municipal requirements for base of operations
  • WorkSafeBC: Registration required if hiring employees (guides)

Action Items:

  1. Consult lawyer specializing in adventure tourism for incorporation and liability structure
  2. Register business name and secure domain
  3. Set up business banking accounts
  4. Apply for GST/PST accounts with CRA and BC government
  5. Secure appropriate business insurance before first trip

2. The Experience

What Guests Receive

Duration: 2 days, 1 night (typical departure Saturday 8am, return Sunday 5pm)

Included in Base Price ($399/person):

  • Guided off-road driving instruction and trail navigation
  • Toyota 4x4 vehicle with rooftop tent setup
  • All camping gear (sleeping bags, pads, pillows, headlamps)
  • All meals: lunch Day 1, dinner Day 1, breakfast Day 2, lunch Day 2
  • Campfire setup and wilderness storytelling
  • Safety briefing and equipment orientation
  • Recovery gear and emergency communication
  • Fuel for the trip
  • Parking at Vancouver meeting point
  • Professional photos of the trip (shared via online gallery)

Not Included:

  • Transportation to/from Vancouver meeting point
  • Alcohol (guests may bring their own within limits)
  • Personal items (clothing, toiletries, medications)
  • Travel insurance
  • Optional tips for guide

Sample Itinerary: 2-Day Squamish Valley Experience

Day 1: Saturday

  • 7:30am: Meet at designated Vancouver parking area
  • 8:00am: Depart for Squamish (1.5 hours)
  • 9:30am: Arrival, safety briefing, vehicle orientation, gear check
  • 10:00am: Begin off-road trail (Callaghan Forest Service Road or similar)
  • 10:00am–1:00pm: Off-road driving instruction: water crossings, rocky terrain, steep descents, recovery techniques
  • 1:00pm: Trailside lunch (sandwiches, snacks, hot drinks)
  • 2:00pm–5:00pm: Continue trail to campsite, practice skills, wildlife spotting
  • 5:00pm: Arrive at wilderness campsite, setup rooftop tents
  • 6:00pm: Dinner preparation and campfire (BBQ or camp stove meal)
  • 7:30pm: Evening around campfire: storytelling, stargazing, trail tales
  • 10:00pm: Retire to rooftop tents

Day 2: Sunday

  • 7:00am: Sunrise coffee and breakfast prep
  • 8:00am: Breakfast (hot meal: eggs, bacon, pancakes or oatmeal bar)
  • 9:00am: Break camp, pack gear
  • 10:00am: Morning trail drive (different route or advanced techniques)
  • 12:00pm: Trailside lunch
  • 1:00pm–3:00pm: Return drive with photo stops at scenic viewpoints
  • 3:30pm: Vehicle cleanup and debrief at staging area
  • 5:00pm: Arrive back at Vancouver meeting point

Experience Variations

Difficulty Levels:

  • Beginner-Friendly (Standard): Suitable for those with driver's license and no off-road experience
  • Intermediate (Seasonal Offering): More technical trails, steeper terrain, advanced recovery techniques
  • Winter Experience ($449/person): Snow trails, winter camping skills, cold-weather gear included

Private Group Options:

  • Custom itineraries for corporate groups (team-building focus)
  • Multi-vehicle convoys for larger private groups (8–10 people, 2 vehicles)
  • Custom celebration packages (birthdays, anniversaries)

What Makes It Special

  1. Hands-On Learning: Guests actually drive (with instructor guidance), unlike passenger-only ATV tours
  2. Real Wilderness: Crown land backcountry camping, not campgrounds—true immersion
  3. Small Groups: Maximum 5 guests creates intimate, personalized experience
  4. All-Inclusive: Zero logistics for guests—show up, adventure, go home
  5. Rooftop Tents: Unique "cool factor" and comfort vs. ground tents
  6. Local Expertise: Guides share BC-specific trail knowledge, history, ecology
  7. Photo Documentation: Professional trip photos shared digitally

  • Motivation: Authentic BC wilderness experience beyond typical tourist activities
  • Pain Points: Limited time in Vancouver; wants unique story to share back home
  • Decision Trigger: Pre-trip research; hotel concierge recommendation; TripAdvisor

Customer Demographics

  • Age: 25–55 (core: 30–45)
  • Income: $60K–$150K household
  • Gender: 55% male, 45% female (though varies by season and marketing)
  • Group Size: 2–3 people average (couples or small friend groups)
  • Geographic: 60% Greater Vancouver, 25% Pacific Northwest US, 15% other/international

Customer Psychographics

  • Values experiences over material possessions
  • Environmentally conscious (but not hardcore backpackers)
  • Active social media users (Instagram, TikTok)
  • Seeks "authentic" but not uncomfortable (glamping, not roughing it)
  • Willing to pay premium for convenience and expertise
  • Interested in skill-building and learning

Market Size & Growth

Total Addressable Market (TAM):

  • Vancouver overnight visitors: 11.27M annually (2024)
  • Estimated 5% interested in adventure tourism: 563,500 people

Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM):

  • Adventure seekers willing to book guided multi-day experiences: ~50,000 annually

Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM - Year 1):

  • Target capture: 40–80 bookings = 120–300 guests (0.24–0.60% of SAM)
  • Conservative, achievable with effective marketing and review-building

  • BC adventure tourism revenue grew from $10.0B (2022) to $10.7B (2023): ~7% annual growth
  • Vancouver visitor spending grew 13% year-over-year (2023–2024)

Seasonal Demand

Peak Season (June–August): 40–50% of annual revenue

  • Ideal weather, longest daylight, school summer break, peak tourist season
  • Premium pricing, highest demand, likely sell-outs

Shoulder Season (April–May, September–October): 35–40% of annual revenue

  • Good weather, lower crowds, fall colors (Sept–Oct)
  • Moderate pricing, solid demand from locals

Off-Peak (November–March): 10–15% of annual revenue

  • Wet/cold weather, shorter days, lower tourist volume
  • Options: Winter premium experience ($449) OR discount pricing ($299) OR seasonal closure
  • Use for vehicle maintenance, content creation, marketing planning

Recommended Annual Calendar:

  • April–October: Primary operating season (28 weeks)
  • November–December: Selective winter trips + holiday gift marketing
  • January–March: Minimal operations, maintenance, planning for next season

7. Operations Plan

Vehicle Preparation & Maintenance

Vehicle Requirements:

  • Base Vehicle: Toyota 4Runner, Tacoma, or similar (reliable, known off-road capability)
  • Modifications Needed:
    • Rooftop tent (CVT, iKamper, or similar): $2,500–$4,000
    • All-terrain tires: $1,200–$1,800
    • Lift kit (optional, improves clearance): $1,500–$3,000
    • Recovery gear: winch or Hi-Lift jack, tow straps, d-rings, shovel: $800–$1,500
    • Roof rack and storage system: $600–$1,200
    • Auxiliary lighting (off-road lights): $300–$600
    • First aid kit, fire extinguisher, emergency supplies: $200–$400

Maintenance Schedule:

  • Pre-trip inspection (every trip): Tire pressure, fluids, lights, recovery gear check
  • Post-trip cleaning (every trip): Exterior wash, interior vacuum, tent cleaning/drying
  • Oil change: Every 5,000 km or 3 months (whichever first)
  • Tire rotation: Every 8,000 km
  • Brake inspection: Every 6 months
  • Suspension check: Every 10,000 km (off-road accelerates wear)
  • Deep clean & maintenance: Monthly (alignment, undercarriage inspection)

Budget:

  • Regular maintenance: $200–$300/month
  • Unexpected repairs: $1,000–$2,000/year reserve
  • Fuel: $100–$150/trip (budgeted into pricing)

Insurance:

  • Commercial auto insurance: $3,000–$6,000/year per vehicle (ICBC)
  • Must disclose commercial use; personal auto insurance is insufficient

Route Planning & Safety Protocols

Route Selection Criteria:

  • Accessibility: Forest service roads or Crown land trails within 2–3 hours of Vancouver
  • Difficulty: Appropriate for beginner/intermediate drivers (avoid extreme technical sections)
  • Scenic Value: Views, water features, wildlife potential
  • Campsite: Legal camping location with space for 3 tents, fire pit, privacy
  • Safety: Cell/satellite coverage for emergency communication, known trail conditions
  • Permits: Confirm Crown land use or obtain necessary permits

Pre-Trip Planning (Week Before):

  • Check weather forecast (3 weather sources for reliability)
  • Confirm trail conditions (check recent trip reports, BC4WD forums)
  • Download offline maps (Gaia GPS, onX Offroad)
  • Pre-drive route if new trail (scouting trip)
  • Confirm guest count and dietary restrictions
  • Prep food and gear
  • Charge satellite communicator

Day-Of Safety Protocols:

  • Vehicle pre-trip inspection (tires, fluids, gear)
  • Guest safety briefing (30 min): vehicle operation, trail hazards, communication signals, wildlife safety, Leave No Trace principles
  • Buddy system for hiking/exploring at camp
  • Daily check-in with emergency contact (text/satellite message)
  • Weather monitoring and contingency plans (alternate routes, early return)

Emergency Protocols:

  • Vehicle breakdown: Recovery gear on-board, backup tow service on standby (BCAA or private)
  • Guest injury: First aid kit, guide trained in Wilderness First Aid (recommended certification), satellite communication to call emergency services
  • Severe weather: Emergency shelter, ability to bug out if necessary
  • Wildlife encounter: Bear spray accessible, protocols briefed pre-trip

Leave No Trace Practices:

  • Pack out all waste (including food scraps)
  • Use designated fire pits or fire pans (check fire bans)
  • Toilet facilities: Pack-out systems or dig catholes 200+ feet from water
  • Minimize impact: Stay on trails, avoid damaging vegetation
  • Respect wildlife: Store food properly, observe from distance

  2. Respond within 4 hours (during business hours) with trip options and availability
  3. Send booking link (Acuity calendar)

Insurance & Liability

Required Insurance Coverage:

  1. Commercial Auto Insurance (ICBC):
  • Coverage: Vehicle used for commercial purposes carrying paying passengers
    • Cost: $3,000–$6,000/year per vehicle
    • Must disclose nature of business to insurer
  1. Commercial General Liability (CGL):
  • Coverage: Minimum $2M (recommended $5M for adventure tourism)
    • Protects against guest injury claims, property damage
    • Cost: $2,000–$4,000/year
    • Source: Brokers specializing in adventure/outdoor recreation (e.g., MEC Insurance, Capri Insurance)
  1. Optional but Recommended:
  • Excess Liability / Umbrella Policy: Additional $3M–$5M coverage
    • Equipment Insurance: Coverage for rooftop tents, camping gear, recovery equipment
    • Business Interruption Insurance: Covers lost income if vehicle is damaged and can't operate

Legal Liability Protection:

  • Signed Waivers: Required from all guests before every trip
    • Assumption of risk, release of liability, indemnification clauses
    • Have lawyer specializing in adventure tourism review waiver language
    • Store digitally via Acuity/booking system (legally binding in BC if properly executed)
  • Incorporation: Protects personal assets from business liabilities

Risk Mitigation:

  • Maintain detailed trip logs (vehicle condition, weather, incidents)
  • Photo/video documentation of vehicle condition before/after trips
  • Incident reporting protocol: Document everything in writing immediately
  • Regular staff training (if hiring guides) on safety and emergency procedures

Permits & Regulations (British Columbia)

Required Permits/Licenses:

  1. BC Crown Land Commercial Recreation Tenure:
  • Required for operating commercial tours on Crown land
    • Apply through BC's Lands Branch (FrontCounterBC)
    • Timeline: 6–12 months (apply early!)
    • Cost: Application fee + annual tenure fees (varies by area and activity)
    • This is non-negotiable—do not operate without it
  1. Business License:
  • Required from municipality where business is based (e.g., City of Vancouver business license)
    • Cost: $100–$200/year
    • Renewal annually
  1. GST/PST Registration:
  • Register for GST if revenue exceeds $30,000/year (likely by mid-Year 1)
    • Collect 5% GST on tour pricing
    • Provincial Sales Tax (PST) may apply to certain services—consult accountant
  1. Food Service Permit (if applicable):
  • BC food safety regulations apply if providing meals commercially
    • May require FoodSafe certification
    • Confirm requirements with BC Health Authority (rules for outdoor/mobile food service)
  1. WorkSafeBC Registration:
  • Required if hiring employees (guides)
    • Workers' compensation coverage
    • Cost: Based on payroll and industry classification

Regulatory Considerations:

  • Fire Bans: Monitor BC Wildfire Service for fire bans (affects campfire component of experience)
  • Trail Closures: Check for seasonal closures, logging activity, or restricted areas
  • Wildlife Regulations: Respect wildlife management areas, seasonal restrictions
  • Leave No Trace: Adhere to all backcountry camping regulations

Recommended Timeline:

  • Month -6 (before launch): Apply for Crown land tenure
  • Month -3: Secure all insurance policies
  • Month -2: Register business, obtain business license, register for GST/PST
  • Month -1: Confirm all permits in place before first trip
