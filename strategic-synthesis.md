## 1. Executive Summary

There is a clear gap in the Vancouver adventure tourism market: **nobody is combining guided off-road driving with curated overnight camping as a single bookable experience.** Existing operators are either day-trip ATV tours ($199–$339/person) or self-drive overlanding rentals ($175–$305/night) — nobody is doing the full-service, guided, overnight off-road package within striking distance of Vancouver. This is a premium experience that can command $350–$500/person for a 2-day trip, targeting small groups of 2–5 guests who want adventure without the hassle of owning gear or planning routes. The market is real: BC adventure tourism generates $10.7B annually, Vancouver sees 11M+ overnight visitors, and the "experience economy" trend is strongly in your favor.

## 2. Market Position Assessment

### Where You Fit
You occupy **white space** in the competitive landscape. The market breaks into three buckets, and you sit between all of them:

| Segment | Who's There | What They're Missing |
|---|---|---|
| Day-trip ATV/buggy tours | Canadian Wilderness Adventures, Squamish Adventure, Vancouver Outdoor Adventures | No overnight. No "real" driving — it's a theme park ride. |
| Self-drive overland rentals | Maple Overland ($225/night), FarOut Wilderness ($175–$305/day) | No guide. Customer does all the work. Intimidating for beginners. |
| Multi-day guided tours | Western Red Adventures (Vancouver Island only) | Wrong geography — they're on the Island, not near Vancouver. |

### Unique Value Proposition
**"The only guided off-road driving + overnight camping experience within 2 hours of Vancouver."**

This is not an ATV tour. This is not a rental. You provide: real off-road driving in Toyota vehicles → guided by an experienced driver → to a curated campsite → with rooftop tents, meals, and gear handled. The guest shows up, drives, camps, and goes home with stories. Zero logistics on their end.

### Competitive Advantages
1. **First-mover in a defined niche** — nobody else is doing this exact thing near Vancouver.
2. **Low capital barrier** — Toyota vehicles + rooftop tents vs. building a lodge or buying ATVs.
3. **High perceived value** — combines two premium experiences (off-road driving + glamping) into one.
4. **Small group intimacy** — max 5 guests = premium feel, strong word-of-mouth.
5. **Year-round potential** — winter off-roading + snow camping is a real product (with right positioning).

### Competitive Disadvantages
1. **No brand recognition** — starting from zero in a market where Canadian Wilderness Adventures has 5,800 reviews.
2. **Weather dependency** — BC weather can wreck trips, and you can't control it.
3. **Capacity constraint** — max 5 guests/trip caps revenue per outing.
4. **Vehicle maintenance costs** — off-road driving is hard on vehicles. Budget accordingly.

### Recommended Positioning
**Premium adventure experience, not budget tour.** Position above the day-trip ATV crowd and above self-drive rentals. You are selling an *experience*, not a rental and not a ride. Closest comparable: high-end guided fishing or heli-skiing — small groups, expert guides, all-inclusive, premium pricing.

## 3. Go-to-Market Strategy

### Top 3 Customer Acquisition Channels

**1. Instagram (Organic + Reels) — Start here, day one.**
This product is inherently visual. Off-road footage, rooftop tent sunrises, campfire dinners in BC backcountry — this content sells itself. 70% of effort on Reels for reach, 20% on feed for depth, 10% on Stories for trust. Post 3–4 Reels/week minimum. This is your storefront before you have a storefront.

**2. Google Business Profile + Local SEO — The trust foundation.**
Free, immediate, and critical. Claim GBP, get your first 10+ five-star reviews from soft-launch guests, and own "off road tours Vancouver" in local search. This is the lowest-cost, highest-trust channel available.

**3. Micro-influencer seeding — Trade trips for content.**
Invite 3–5 local micro-influencers (5K–50K followers) on midweek trips in exchange for content. Your cost is fuel + food. Their content becomes your social proof and reaches their niche outdoor audience. This is how you build credibility before you have 100 reviews.

### Launch vs. Growth Phase

**Launch (Months 1–6): Awareness & proof.**
- Spend 15–20% of projected revenue on marketing.
- 40% on content production (you need professional photos/video — this is non-negotiable).
- 30% on social ads to jumpstart reach.
- 20% on influencer/barter trips.
- 10% on tools (booking platform, email, Canva).

**Growth (Months 6–18): Efficiency & scale.**
- Drop to 10–12% of revenue.
- 50% on paid ads (scale what's working).
- 25% on SEO/content (blog posts compound over time).
- 15% on partnership commissions (hotels, concierges).
- 10% on email/retention (repeat bookings, referrals).

### Budget Allocation (Launch Phase)
Assuming $3,000–$5,000/month marketing budget at launch:
- Content production: $1,200–$2,000
- Social/Google ads: $900–$1,500
- Influencer trips: $600–$1,000 (mostly cost-of-goods)
- Tools & software: $300–$500

### Key Marketing Risks
1. **Content quality risk** — Bad photos = no bookings. Invest in a shooter for the first 3 trips minimum.
2. **Review velocity risk** — You need reviews fast. If first guests have a bad experience, it's very hard to recover.
3. **Seasonality risk** — Marketing spend in winter may not convert if you don't have a compelling winter product.
4. **Platform dependency** — Instagram algorithm changes could tank organic reach overnight. Diversify to SEO early.

## 4. Technology Recommendation

### Verdict: Buy first, build later.

**Launch on Squarespace Commerce Basic ($45/mo) + Acuity Scheduling Growing ($36/mo) = ~$972 CAD/year.**

Rationale: You are pre-revenue. Every hour spent building a custom website is an hour not spent running trips and building your brand. Squarespace + Acuity gets you live in 2 weeks with:
- Professional templates designed for experiential businesses
- Real-time booking with group capacity (up to 5 guests)
- 30% deposits + automatic balance collection via Stripe
- Automated reminders, waiver/intake forms, coupon codes
- Built-in blog, email campaigns, and analytics
- Canadian-friendly Stripe/PayPal payments with CAD pricing

**Do NOT build custom.** Not at this stage. A custom Next.js build is $3,000–$8,000+ and 6–10 weeks with a freelancer, or 120–160 hours of your own time. That time is better spent on the first 20 trips.

### Phased Technology Roadmap

| Phase | Timeline | Platform | Trigger to Move |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Phase 0** | Weeks 1–2 | Squarespace + Acuity + Stripe | — |
| **Phase 1** | Months 1–6 | Add blog content, social proof integrations, Google Analytics | — |
| **Phase 2** | Months 6–12 | Evaluate Checkfront or Rezdy if you need OTA distribution, reseller logins, or richer manifests | 100+ bookings or OTA partnerships |
| **Phase 3** | Year 2+ | Migrate to WordPress/Webflow + Checkfront/Rezdy for full control | 400+ bookings/year or multi-vehicle fleet |

### Budget Implications
- Year 1 total platform cost: ~$972 + Stripe fees (2.9% + $0.30/transaction)
- Year 2+ if you stay on Squarespace: same ~$972
- Migration to WordPress/Webflow + Checkfront: $2,000–$5,000 one-time + ~$2,400/year ongoing

## 5. Pricing Strategy

### Recommended Price Points

| Product | Price (CAD) | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **2-Day/1-Night Experience (per person)** | **$399** | Sits above day tours ($199–$339) and above rental rates ($175–$305/night). Justified by all-inclusive value: driving, guide, camp, gear, meals. |
| **Private Group Booking (up to 5)** | **$1,595** (= $319/person at 5 pax) | Group discount baked in. Encourages full-capacity bookings. Comparable to Vancouver Outdoor Adventures' $800/group for a day trip — you're offering 2x the experience. |
| **Premium Add-On: Winter/Snow Experience** | **$449/person** | Higher price for higher difficulty, rarer experience, and additional gear costs. |

### Pricing Model
**Per-person pricing as the default**, with a private group flat rate as the upsell. This is cleaner than per-vehicle or tiered pricing. Guests understand per-person pricing intuitively.

- **Minimum booking:** 2 guests (otherwise unit economics don't work at $399/person)
- **Solo travelers:** $599 solo surcharge or waitlist to join an existing group
- **Deposit:** 30% at booking ($120/person), balance auto-charged 7 days before departure

### Seasonal Pricing

| Season | Pricing | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Peak (Jun–Sep)** | Full price ($399/person) | No discounts needed — demand should carry. |
| **Shoulder (Apr–May, Oct)** | 10–15% off ($349/person) | Drive bookings in shoulder months. |
| **Off-peak (Nov–Mar)** | Winter experience at $449 OR promotional rate at $299 | Either premium winter product or discounted to fill capacity. |
| **Midweek** | 15% discount ($339/person) | Incentivize Wed–Thu departures to improve utilization. |

## 6. Risk Assessment

### Top 5 Business Risks

| # | Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Weather cancellations erode revenue and reputation** | High | Clear cancellation tiers (full refund >14 days, 50% at 7–13 days). Offer free rebooking for weather cancellations. Build "rain contingency" into the experience (tarps, covered cooking, campfire under cover). |
| 2 | **Vehicle damage/breakdown on trail** | High | Carry recovery gear on every trip. Budget $5,000–$10,000/year for maintenance/repairs. Have a backup vehicle arrangement. Get proper commercial vehicle insurance. |
| 3 | **Guest injury/liability** | High | Require signed waivers before every trip. Carry commercial general liability insurance ($2M+ minimum). Brief guests on safety protocols. Consider additional umbrella coverage. |
| 4 | **Slow initial demand / can't fill trips** | Medium | Keep fixed costs low (Squarespace, not custom build). Run midweek trips for influencers. Offer early-bird pricing. Don't quit your day job until you're consistently filling 3+ trips/month. |
| 5 | **Regulatory/permitting issues** | Medium | Research BC Crown land use permits, commercial recreation tenure requirements, and municipal bylaws for camping. Get ahead of this — don't get shut down after launch. |

### Regulatory & Insurance Considerations (BC)
- **Commercial Recreation Tenure:** Operating commercial tours on BC Crown land requires a tenure or license from the Province. Apply through BC's Lands Branch. This is not optional.
- **Commercial Vehicle Insurance:** ICBC commercial auto insurance is required for any vehicle carrying paying passengers. Budget $3,000–$6,000/year per vehicle.
- **Commercial General Liability (CGL):** Minimum $2M coverage. Adventure tourism operators in BC typically carry $5M. Get quotes from brokers who specialize in adventure/outdoor recreation.
- **WorkSafeBC:** If you hire guides, you need WorkSafeBC coverage.
- **Food safety:** If you're providing meals, check BC food safety regulations for outdoor/mobile food service.

### Weather & Seasonal Risk
- BC's shoulder and off-peak seasons (Oct–Apr) bring rain, snow, and shorter days. This limits trip frequency and increases cancellation risk.
- Mitigation: Build a genuine winter product (snow off-roading + winter camping) or accept seasonal revenue swings and budget accordingly. Don't try to force summer pricing on a winter product that isn't differentiated.

### Vehicle Maintenance & Liability
- Off-road driving accelerates wear on suspension, tires, undercarriage, and drivetrain. Budget 2–3x normal maintenance costs.
- Carry a recovery kit (winch, tow straps, shovel, tire repair) on every trip.
- Document vehicle condition before/after every trip with photos. This protects you in insurance claims.

## 7. Recommended Path Forward

### Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1–3)

**Objective:** Get live, get reviews, validate pricing.

| Week | Action |
|---|---|
| 1–2 | Set up Squarespace + Acuity. Claim Google Business Profile. Set up Stripe. Finalize one core 2-day/1-night itinerary. |
| 3–4 | Run 2–3 "friends & family" soft-launch trips at cost. Collect Google reviews, photos, and video. |
| 5–6 | Launch publicly at $399/person. Start Instagram posting (3–4 Reels/week). Turn on Google Ads at $500/month on high-intent keywords. |
| 7–8 | Host 2–3 micro-influencer trips. Refine itinerary based on feedback. |
| 9–12 | Optimize: What content converts? What's the actual CAC? Adjust pricing if needed. Apply for commercial recreation tenure if not already done. |

**Phase 1 Milestone:** 15–20 paid bookings. 20+ Google reviews at 4.8+ stars. Instagram at 1,000+ followers.

### Phase 2: Growth (Months 3–6)

**Objective:** Fill trips consistently, diversify channels, add a second product.

- Scale paid ads on channels that are converting (likely Instagram + Google).
- Launch hotel/hostel partnership program (10–15% concierge commission).
- Add a second itinerary (different route/difficulty level or a winter product).
- List on Viator/TripAdvisor Experiences to test OTA demand (accept the 20–30% commission as customer acquisition cost).
- Start blog content for SEO (target 2 posts/month on "off-road trails Vancouver," "camping near Squamish," etc.).
- Evaluate Checkfront/Rezdy if OTA distribution or group manifesting becomes a pain point.

**Phase 2 Milestone:** 8–12 trips/month in peak season. Revenue run rate of $5,000–$8,000/month. At least one hotel partnership generating referrals.

### Phase 3: Scale (Months 6–12)

**Objective:** Maximize capacity utilization, consider second vehicle, build moat.

- If demand justifies it, add a second vehicle to run parallel trips or back-to-back days.
- Hire a part-time guide to run trips you're not on.
- Migrate to a more robust booking system (Checkfront or Rezdy) for multi-vehicle inventory management.
- Build corporate/team-building packages ($2,000–$3,000 for private group experiences).
- Create gift card/voucher program for holiday season (Nov–Dec push).
- Evaluate expansion: new routes, 3-day trips, partnerships with Indigenous tourism operators.

**Phase 3 Milestone:** 15–20 trips/month in peak season. Revenue run rate of $10,000–$15,000/month. Second vehicle operational. Corporate clients representing 20%+ of bookings.

### Key Decision Points

| Decision | Trigger | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| Add second vehicle | Turning away 5+ bookings/month | Buy, don't lease. Toyota holds value. |
| Hire a guide | Running 12+ trips/month | Start part-time. Train extensively before solo trips. |
| Switch booking platform | OTA distribution needed or 100+ bookings/year | Move to Checkfront ($149/mo) or Rezdy ($49–$99/mo). |
| Rebuild website | 400+ bookings/year | WordPress or Webflow + dedicated booking system. |
| Raise prices | Consistently selling out 2+ weeks in advance | You're underpriced. Raise $50/person and monitor. |
| Add winter product | Demand exists OR summer revenue needs smoothing | Price at $449+. Requires additional gear investment. |

---

*Bottom line: You have a real opportunity in a defined gap. The playbook is simple — launch lean, get reviews fast, let the visuals sell the experience, and scale only when demand proves it. Don't overbuild anything until you've run 50 trips.*
