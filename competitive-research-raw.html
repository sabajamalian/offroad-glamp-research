Direct‑competitor landscape takeaways:

  • Most Vancouver‑area off‑road products are day‑trips (ATV/buggy tours) with pricing clustered around $199–$339 per rider/buggy or $800 per private group day.
  • Overlanding in BC is mostly self‑drive rentals (Maple Overland, FarOut Wilderness) or multi‑day convoy tours on Vancouver Island (Western Red Adventures), leaving a gap for guided overnight experiences near Vancouver.

  • Online booking platforms: Operators and tours are widely listed on TripAdvisor, Viator, and GetYourGuide (e.g., Vancouver 4WD/ATV tours and Vancouver hiking tours). TripAdvisor 4WD tours · Viator 4WD tours · GetYourGuide

5) Gaps & Opportunities

  1. Guided overnight off‑road + camping is scarce near Vancouver. Current direct competitors are either day‑only ATV/buggy tours or self‑drive rentals; guided multi‑day options are mainly Vancouver Island‑based. This leaves room for a Vancouver‑centric overnight guided off‑road + camping package. Day tours · Self‑drive rentals · Vancouver Island tours
  2. Pricing gap for mid‑range multi‑day experiences. Day tours run ~$199–$339 per rider/buggy, while rentals are $175–$305/day. There is little in‑market pricing for 2‑day guided, all‑inclusive overland experiences near Vancouver. ATV pricing · Buggy pricing ref · Rental pricing
  3. Experience gap: operators don't combine hands‑on off‑road driving with a curated overnight camp (gear setup, meals, stargazing, local storytelling). This is a clear differentiation point versus glamping and day‑tour competition. Glamping market
  4. Underserved segments: small groups seeking private, guided, beginner‑friendly off‑road plus comfort‑level camping could be targeted with bundled pricing and transportation from Vancouver.
