## Project Context
- **Owner:** Saba
- **Business:** Off-road driving experience + car camping in Vancouver BC area
- **Stack:** Research & business planning (document deliverables)
- **Concept:** Taking up to 5 guests outside Vancouver BC for off-road driving with Toyota vehicles, overnight stays with rooftop tents
- **Deliverables:** Competitive Analysis document and Business Plan document

## Learnings

### Document Structure Decisions (March 2025)

**Competitive Analysis Structure:**
- Led with Executive Summary to provide standalone overview for busy readers
- Used comparison tables extensively for scanability (competitors, pricing, channels)
- Created text-based positioning map to visualize market gaps
- Included both direct and indirect competitors for complete picture
- Ended with actionable recommendations, not just analysis
- Key insight: The "gaps & opportunities" section proved most valuable for strategy

**Business Plan Structure:**
- Followed traditional business plan format but made it readable (not academic)
- Each major section includes "Recommended Path" or actionable next steps
- Used tables for financial projections (three scenarios: conservative, moderate, optimistic)
- Implementation timeline broken into phases with specific milestones
- Risk analysis presented as mitigation strategies, not just fears
- Emphasized "launch lean, scale smart" philosophy throughout

**Writing Approach:**
- Bold key numbers and recommendations for executive scanning
- Mixed prose with tables/lists for varied reading experience
- Used real data from research (not generic advice)
- Maintained professional tone while staying conversational
- Cross-referenced between documents to avoid duplication
- Included "why" behind every recommendation, not just "what"

**Key Themes Across Both Documents:**
1. **First-mover advantage** in guided overnight off-road near Vancouver
2. **Launch lean:** Squarespace not custom build, validate before scaling
3. **Content quality is non-negotiable:** Product is visual, Instagram is storefront
4. **Reviews = competitive moat:** First to 50+ reviews owns the space
5. **Premium positioning:** $399/person justified by all-inclusive value

### What Worked Well
- Starting with Mal's strategic synthesis gave clear direction
- Using real competitor pricing created credible financial projections
- Breaking business plan into implementation phases made it actionable
- SWOT analysis provided balanced view (not just cheerleading)
- Extensive research from Zoe, Inara, and Kaylee meant rich source material

### What Was Challenging
- Balancing comprehensiveness with readability (both docs are long but necessary)
- Avoiding duplication between competitive analysis and business plan
- Presenting financial projections without being overly optimistic or pessimistic
- Structuring operations plan with enough detail but not overwhelming
- Regulatory/legal sections required careful language (not legal advice)
