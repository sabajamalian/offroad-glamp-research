# mal — Session History

## Project Context
- **Owner:** Saba
- **Business:** Off-road driving experience + car camping in Vancouver BC area
- **Stack:** Research & business planning (document deliverables)
- **Concept:** Taking up to 5 guests outside Vancouver BC for off-road driving with Toyota vehicles, overnight stays with rooftop tents
- **Deliverables:** Competitive Analysis document and Business Plan document

## Learnings
- **Positioning:** The business occupies clear white space — no competitor combines guided off-road driving + curated overnight camping near Vancouver. Closest comparables are day-trip ATV tours or self-drive overlanding rentals.
- **Pricing anchor:** Recommended $399/person for 2-day/1-night, with $1,595 private group rate (up to 5). Sits above day tours ($199–$339) and above rental rates ($175–$305/night). Premium positioning is justified by all-inclusive guided experience.
- **Technology:** Squarespace + Acuity is the right launch stack (~$972/year). Custom builds are premature pre-revenue. Migrate to WordPress/Webflow + Checkfront/Rezdy at 400+ bookings/year.
- **Go-to-market:** Instagram Reels, Google Business Profile, and micro-influencer seeding are the top 3 launch channels. Content quality (professional photos/video) is the single biggest marketing risk.
- **Critical regulatory item:** BC commercial recreation tenure is required for operating on Crown land. ICBC commercial vehicle insurance is non-negotiable. These must be secured before launch.
- **Revenue targets:** Phase 1 goal is 15–20 paid bookings in first 3 months. Phase 3 target is 15–20 trips/month in peak season at $10K–$15K/month run rate.
- **Key strategic principle:** Launch lean, get reviews fast, scale only when demand proves it. Don't overbuild anything until 50 trips completed.
